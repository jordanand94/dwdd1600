Now Playing

  • The Manchurian Candidate (1962) 12:00PM 2:50PM 7:00PM 9:30PM

Closes after June 13th

Starring Frank Sinatra in the leading role, this film follows a Korean War veteran who returns home to his notable political family in the United States. Unknown to no one back home is the fact that this man has been brainwashed to become an assassin by communists and will soon become a part of an international communist conspiracy. Considered one of the great political thrillers of all-time, revisit this classic film on the big screen!

  • About Schmidt (2002) 12:30PM 3:45PM 7:00PM

Closes after June 13th

Jack Nicholson shines brightly in this film from director Alexander Payne, playing a recently retired man who goes on a roadtrip to prevent his daughter from marrying a man he deems unfit to be with her. Along the way, he encounters people and events that give him a new outlook on his life and the time he has left to live. An underrated gem with great supportive performances from the likes of Kathy Bates and Dermot Mulroney, this is one you won't want to miss. Alexander Payne will be in house for the June 11th showing to talk about his film! One night only following the 7:00PM screening!

  • The Godfather (1972) 12:15PM 4:00PM 8:00PM

Closes after June 20th

Considered one of the great cinematic masterpieces of our time, with legendary performances by Marlon Brando and Al Pacino, this film centers on the Corleone crime family and the reluctance of one Michael Corleone to play his role in it, until he ultimately has no choice. Directed by the great Francis Ford Coppola, come see this beloved film on the big screen while you still can!

  • Kramer vs. Kramer (1979) 12:00PM 3:00PM 7:30PM

Closes after June 20th

A notable depiction of divorce and the nuanced ways it can shift family dynamics, Dustin Hoffman and Meryl Streep give Oscar-winning performances as parents in the midst of divorce and their battle for custody of their son. This film holds a timeless place in history for its brutally honest portrayal of your average American family.

  • The African Queen (1951) 12:30PM 4:15PM 7:30PM

Closes after June 27th

Humphrey Bogart and Katherine Hepburn partner in this film that holds a special place in the romantic adventure genre. Bogart plays a Canadian steamer captain who allows an English missionary played by Hepburn to aboard his boat The African Queen, a boat that is a safe haven in the chaos of WW1. Together, they aim to detroy a German gunboat and eventually fall in love.
